About the Wormhole

The Wormhole (Valley Library and Remote) is a Physics Collaboration and Help Center. It is an informal space to work on physics with peers under the guidance of experienced Assistants. Complete instructions are available in Wormhole Instructions.

Requesting Assistance

If you or your group needs help with a physics question, send a request to the queue. In person, use the box on your table. Online, use the request form. Only one person in the group needs to enter the request once for each question. Help is provided in the order requests are received, and you can track your place using the Live Queue.

Wormhole Assistants

Wormhole assistants are graduate students in physics, physics majors, or other undergraduates who took one of the introductory physics sequences. They can guide you through course work, but they may also refer you to another assistant or your instructor when needed.

Virtual Wormhole

  1. Connect to the Virtual Wormhole channel in the Introductory Physics MS Team. Full instructions are in the MS Teams Instructions.
  2. Join the Wormhole Zoom session here (password: Wormhole).

Code of Conduct

Welcome to The Wormhole! To ensure this space remains an effective and accessible learning environment for everyone, we ask all students to uphold the following community standards:

1. Maintain a Respectful and Focused Environment
  • Help maintain a quiet study space: Keep conversations at a low volume and use respectful language.
  • Share the space respectfully: Be mindful of shared work and study space. To support an accessible environment, please avoid strong fragrances and strong-smelling foods, as they may distract others or affect those with allergies or sensitivities.
  • Support the room’s academic purpose: The Wormhole is a dedicated physics resource. During busy periods, please leave seating and table space available for students working on physics coursework.
2. Engage Constructively with Teaching Assistants
  • Work in a learning-focused way: Assistants are here to support your understanding and problem-solving process rather than complete assignments on your behalf. They will help you work through concepts and strengthen your problem-solving skills rather than simply provide final answers.
  • Be patient and courteous: Assistants strive to help as many students as possible. Please be patient while waiting your turn. Additionally, because staff support a range of courses, they may occasionally need a moment to review the details of an assignment before assisting.
3. Leave the Space Ready for the Next Student
  • Treat the space with care: When you have finished studying, please clear your area of trash, erase whiteboards, and leave the workstation tidy for the next student.
